Types of Paint Finishes and Their Characteristics

Flat Paint

Flat paint has a non-reflective surface and is perfect for hiding surface imperfections. Its matte finish makes the pigment more visible and provides a uniform surface. It is a great option for ceilings and walls that don't require frequent cleaning.

Eggshell Paint

Eggshell paint has a slight sheen and a velvety texture that gives walls a subtle glow. It is more durable than flat paint and can be easily cleaned, making it ideal for high-traffic areas. It is a popular choice among both DIYers and professionals.

Pearl Paint

Pearl paint has a medium-gloss finish that is both durable and easy to clean. It provides a subtle shimmer that adds dimension to walls and trims, making it a great option for busy areas. Satin Paint

Satin paint has a soft sheen that is more pronounced than eggshell, making it a popular choice for bathrooms and kitchens. It is more durable and stain-resistant than eggshell, making it easier to clean. Satin paint can be used on walls, trims, and doors.

Semi-Gloss Paint

Semi-gloss paint has a luminous appearance that is perfect for highlighting architectural features, such as doors, millwork, and trims. It is highly durable and easy to clean, making it a great option for high-traffic areas. Semi-gloss paint is more reflective than satin and eggshell finishes.
